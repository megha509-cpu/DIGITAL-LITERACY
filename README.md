##  Project Overview
This project was done as part of the Digital Literacy course where I acted as a "Student Digital Ambassador". The main aim was to understand how to use digital tools properly, create a professional online presence, and also learn about online safety and communication.

Through this project, I explored different platforms like GitHub, LinkedIn, Kaggle, and HackerRank. I also created a quiz, worked on a case study, and learned about cybercrime awareness.



##  Task Summaries

### Task 1 – Infographic
I created a digital literacy infographic using Canva. It explains what digital literacy is and why it is important for students. One difficulty I faced was arranging content in a clean and simple way.



### Task 2 – Digital Portfolio
I created accounts on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. These platforms help in building a professional identity. I plan to use GitHub for projects, LinkedIn for networking, and Kaggle for learning data science.



### Task 3 – Platforms
I used HackerRank to solve a beginner coding problem which helped me understand basic logic. I also created a Google Form quiz on digital literacy and linked it with Google Sheets to collect responses.

### Task 4 – Email & Etiquette
I wrote two professional emails and created a checklist for responsible social media usage. This task helped me understand how important proper communication is in professional settings.



### Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness
I studied UPI fraud as a cybercrime case. I learned how easily people can be tricked online. I also created a prevention checklist to spread awareness and help others stay safe.



## Key Learnings
- Digital presence is important even as a student  
- Small mistakes online can lead to serious problems  
- Communication and awareness are very important  
- Being careful online is more important than being fast
